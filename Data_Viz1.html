Data Analysis:Max Temperature vs. Latitude

This plot represents the maximum measured temperature on January 6 2019 for the 500 random cities chosen. As one would expect, the temperature is higher as we approach the center of the plot which represents the equator. The unexpected result is that even though the cities were randomly selected, the southern hemisphere is so parsely populated that there is much less data, and in particular no data avaialable for greater than 60 deg south. This phenomenon is not so easily noticed on the other plots. Why? Though no statistical analyis was done, common understanding and this visualiztion point to a strong correlation between latitude and temperature. Because the data is so tightly grouped, the rightward skew is easily visible, indicating significatntly more data came from the nothern hemisphere.
